# NGO Financial Performance (2022–2025)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes the financial performance of an NGO over a 4-year period (2022–2025), focusing on revenue sources, expense structure, and overall financial sustainability.

## Objectives
- Identify the main sources of revenue
- Analyze how expenses are distributed
- Evaluate financial sustainability over time
- Detect potential financial risks

## Dataset
The dataset contains financial records including:
- Revenues (membership fees, sponsorships, other income)
- Expenses (personnel, communication, administrative, etc.)
- Time period: 2022–2025

## Data Privacy
All personal and organizational information was anonymized prior to analysis.

## Tools Used
- Tableau (data visualization)
- SQL (data preparation)
- Excel (data cleaning)

## Key Insights

- Membership fees represent ~68% of total revenue, making the organization highly dependent on its members
- Sponsorship contributes ~31% but is inconsistent across years
- Other income sources are negligible (~1%) and do not significantly impact financial sustainability
- Personnel costs account for the largest share of expenses (~61%)
- The organization recorded a deficit in 2025, indicating potential financial risk

## Conclusion

The organization is financially stable in most years but relies heavily on membership revenue.  
A decline in membership or an increase in costs could negatively impact long-term sustainability.
